Introduction to Standard Deviation

Introduction to Standard Devaition

Standard deviation is the measure of dispersion of a set of data from its mean. It measures the absolute variability of a distribution; the higher the dispersion or variability, the greater is the standard deviation and greater will be the magnitude of the deviation of the value from their mean.

Demerits of Standard Deviation

Here are the demerits of using mode as a descriptive statistic for calculations

  1. Its calculation is difficult as it involves many mathematical models and processes.
  2. It is affected very much by the extreme values of a series in as much as the squares of deviations of big items proportionately bigger than the squares of the smaller items.
  3. It cannot be used for comparing the dispersion of two, or more series given in different units
